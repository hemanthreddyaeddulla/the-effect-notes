Glossary

64 terms, each linked back to the episode that introduced it.

Adjusting for
conditioning on background factors so they can't drive the remaining relationship. Ep 11
Alternative explanation
a different data generating process that would produce the same observations. Ep 9
Binary variable
a categorical variable with exactly two options. Ep 3
Binning
grouping a continuous variable into ranges so it can be conditioned on. Ep 6
Binomial distribution
the distribution for a series of yes/no trials, such as coin flips. Ep 5
Categorical variable
mutually exclusive, unordered groups. Ep 3
Causal inference
using data to determine whether one thing causes another, especially without a controlled experiment. Ep 1
Central tendency
a single value representing the centre of a distribution. Ep 4
Coding
converting qualitative material into a countable or categorical variable. Ep 3
Conditional conditional distribution
the distribution of Y given X, within variation already conditioned on Z. Ep 8
Conditional distribution
the distribution of one variable among observations sharing a value of another. Ep 6
Conditional mean
the mean of one variable computed within a value or bin of another. Ep 6
Conditioning
restricting attention to observations at a particular value of a variable. Ep 6
Continuous variable
takes any value in a range; no next value up. Ep 3
Control variable
a variable you condition on in order to remove its explanation from a relationship. Ep 8
Controlling for / adjusting for / matching on
three names for conditioning on a variable and examining the relationship that remains. Ep 8
Correlation
a rescaled, unitless regression slope, running from −1 to 1. Ep 7
Count variable
a whole-number tally, non-negative. Ep 3
Data generating process
the underlying truth in the world that produces your data. Ep 5
Data generating process (DGP)
the set of laws and relationships that determine why the data is what it is. Ep 9
Density
the limit of a histogram as bin width goes to zero; describes likelihood near a point. Ep 4
Distribution
how often each possible value of a variable occurs. Ep 4
Explained part
the conditional mean itself; what the conditioning variable accounts for. Ep 8
Feasibility
whether the data can be obtained and the study permitted at all. Ep 2
Hazard ratio
a measure where higher values mean less healthy, more likely to die sooner. Ep 11
Histogram
a continuous variable cut into bins, with observations counted per bin. Ep 4
Hypothesis testing
the frequentist procedure of rejecting a candidate truth when the data would be sufficiently unlikely under it. Ep 5
Identification
establishing that a particular analysis of your data recovers the specific quantity your question asks about. Ep 1
Identified
the state in which all alternative explanations have been eliminated or removed, so a remaining relationship answers your research question. Ep 10
Independence
the case where conditioning on one variable leaves the other's distribution unchanged. Ep 6
Intercept
the fitted value of Y when X is zero. Ep 7
Interquartile range
the distance from the 25th to the 75th percentile; the percentile-family measure of spread. Ep 4
Line-fitting
summarizing a relationship with a shape whose height at each X gives the conditional mean of Y. Ep 7
LOESS curve
a smooth non-parametric fit produced by rolling a narrow local window along X. Ep 7
Log-normal distribution
a variable that is normally distributed after taking logarithms. Ep 5
Mean
the sum of values divided by the count; the mean-family central tendency. Ep 4
Median
the 50th percentile; the middle observation. Ep 4
Non-parametric regression
fitting without specifying a functional form in advance. Ep 7
Normal distribution
the distribution averages tend to follow. Ep 5
Ordinal variable
categories with a meaningful order but no meaningful spacing. Ep 3
Ordinary least squares (OLS)
linear regression; fits a pre-specified shape, usually a straight line, by best fit. Ep 7
p-value
the probability of your result or something more extreme, assuming the candidate theoretical distribution is correct. Ep 5
Percentile
the value below which a given share of the sample falls. Ep 4
Placebo test
applying your method to a relationship that should be zero under your assumptions; a non-zero result signals something left out. Ep 11
Poisson distribution
the distribution for count data. Ep 5
Population
the whole of what you want to learn about, as opposed to your sample. Ep 5
Qualitative variable
too complex to represent mathematically; must be coded before quantitative use. Ep 3
Randomization
assigning treatment by a random device, so nothing about the observations can influence who is treated. Ep 10
Research design
the plan connecting a research question to an analysis, such that the analysis answers that question. Ep 1
Research question
an answerable question about something observable, chosen so that answering it informs a theory. Ep 2
Residual
the part of an observation not explained by the conditional mean; actual minus expected. Ep 8
Sample distribution
the distribution actually observed in your data, as opposed to in theory. Ep 4
Sampling distribution
the same idea, named for what it varies over: samples. Ep 5
Scale
whether you have the money, time, and people the question would require. Ep 2
Selection
when who ends up in your data depends on the outcome you're measuring, as with insurance switchers. Ep 1
Setting
a real-world situation in which your intended design can actually be applied. Ep 2
Skew
spread concentrated in one direction, producing one long tail. Ep 4
Slope
the change in the conditional mean of Y associated with a one-unit increase in X. Ep 7
Standard deviation
the square root of the variance; the mean-family measure of spread. Ep 4
Theoretical distribution
the distribution a statistic would follow across repeated samples, derived from assumptions about the world. Ep 5
Theory
a claim about why the world works a certain way; contains a because, not just a description. Ep 2
Two-tailed test
a test counting extreme results in both directions rather than one. Ep 5
Variable
a measurement recorded repeatedly across observations. Ep 3
Variance
the mean of squared deviations from the mean. Ep 4
